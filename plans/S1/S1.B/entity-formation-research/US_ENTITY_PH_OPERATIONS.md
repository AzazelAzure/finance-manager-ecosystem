# US entity — primarily serving customers in the Philippines

**Purpose:** Research scaffold for **Branch B** and for **US sister company** options. **Not tax or legal advice.**

## Why this file exists

If PH local ownership is delayed, HitM may still need to **contract, invoice, or receive settlement** for SaaS revenue from users in the Philippines. A **US-formed entity** (commonly LLC or Delaware/Wyoming LLC, or US corporation if investors dictate) is one bridge — feasibility depends on **cost, tax, and PSP KYB**, not formation filing fees alone.

### Foreign branch / “doing business” in the PH vs offshore vendor

Some structures trigger **minimum capital**, **local registration**, or **tax nexus** rules that do **not** apply the same way to a **foreign company selling services from abroad** with only cross-border contracts. Treatment is **activity-specific** (e.g. [RA 7042](https://lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra1991/ra_7042_1991.html) and related rules as amended, plus SEC practice). **Hypothesis only:** a US LLC acting as **offshore licensor / technical vendor** to a Filipino-owned PH operating entity is a different analysis from a **US company registering a Philippine branch** to retail locally. **Confirm with counsel** before assuming any path is open or closed.
