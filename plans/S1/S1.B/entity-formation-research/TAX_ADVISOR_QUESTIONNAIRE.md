# US CPA & Tax Advisor Consultation — Pre-Engagement Questionnaire

**Purpose:** This document outlines the specific questions regarding US taxation, the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (FEIE), and cross-border transfer pricing to discuss with a US CPA or international tax advisor.

## 1. US LLC Taxation & FEIE (Foreign Earned Income Exclusion)

**Context:** HitM is a US citizen residing in the Philippines. The US LLC will act as a technical vendor/IP holder licensing software to the PH operating entity.

*   **Q1.1: LLC Classification:** Given the FEIE, should the US LLC remain a default disregarded entity, or is there a benefit to electing S-Corp or C-Corp status for this specific revenue stream?
*   **Q1.2: Earned vs. Passive Income:** Will the income generated by the US LLC from software licensing/royalties be classified as "earned income" (eligible for FEIE) or "passive income" (ineligible for FEIE)? How does the technical service component alter this classification?
*   **Q1.3: Self-Employment Tax:** As an expat utilizing the FEIE, how will Self-Employment (SE) tax apply to the net earnings of the disregarded US LLC? Does the US-PH tax treaty offer any relief?

## 2. Permanent Establishment (PE) & Nexus

**Context:** The US LLC will have no physical office in the Philippines other than HitM working remotely. It will provide services exclusively to the PH spouse-led entity.

*   **Q2.1: PE Risk:** Does HitM physically working on the code in the Philippines create a Permanent Establishment (PE) for the US LLC, potentially subjecting the US LLC's income to Philippine Corporate Income Tax?
*   **Q2.2: State Nexus (US):** Depending on the state of formation (e.g., Wyoming vs. Delaware vs. home state), what are the ongoing state filing requirements and franchise taxes, considering no physical US presence?

## 3. Transfer Pricing & Intercompany Flows

**Context:** The PH Entity collects PHP revenue from customers. A percentage of this revenue must be paid to the US LLC as a vendor/licensing fee.

*   **Q3.1: Arm's-Length Standards:** What is a defensible, IRS-compliant method for determining the fee split (e.g., 80% to PH Entity, 20% to US LLC)? What documentation (e.g., transfer pricing study) is practically required for a startup at this scale?
*   **Q3.2: Repatriation of Funds:** What is the most tax-efficient method to move HitM's share of the profits from the US LLC to personal accounts in the Philippines, factoring in exchange rates and potential double taxation?
*   **Q3.3: Form 5471 / 8858:** Does the spouse's ownership of the PH Entity trigger any complex foreign entity reporting requirements for HitM (e.g., Form 5471 for Controlled Foreign Corporations) under constructive ownership rules?
