# Philippines — spouse-led entity and later transfer

**Purpose:** Capture **research questions** where the **PH operating entity** may initially be owned or registered by an **eligible spouse**, with HitM participating only through lawful structures (employment, IP license, capital, etc.). **Not legal advice.**

Foreign-equity rules, minimum capital, and Anti-Dummy Law depend on **activity class** and corporate form—see [DECISION_MATRIX.md](./DECISION_MATRIX.md) research note and [SPOUSE_INVOLVEMENT_REQUIREMENTS.md](./SPOUSE_INVOLVEMENT_REQUIREMENTS.md).

## Scenario

- Spouse is **eligible** to register a business in the Philippines (citizenship/residency as applicable).
- HitM contributes labor, product, capital, or IP **only** through arrangements **approved by Philippine counsel** (not informal nominee structures).
- Later, **ownership or economic interest** may transfer or be reshaped when HitM’s status and law allow.

## Questions for PH corporate counsel (checklist)

1. **Vehicle:** Sole proprietorship vs OPC vs stock corp for **PSP KYB** and future transfer.
2. **Foreign participation:** When and how HitM may become **stockholder, director, officer**, or **contractor** without violating Anti-Dummy or foreign-capital rules.
3. **Transfer mechanics:** Share transfer vs new holding company — tax and SEC documentation.
4. **Matrimonial property:** Effect on transfers and consent.
5. **Interim governance:** Employment vs **B2B license** — written boundaries; see [SPOUSE_INVOLVEMENT_REQUIREMENTS.md](./SPOUSE_INVOLVEMENT_REQUIREMENTS.md).
