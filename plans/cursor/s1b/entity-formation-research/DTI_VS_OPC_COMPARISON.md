# DTI Sole Proprietorship vs. One Person Corporation (OPC)

**Purpose:** This document outlines the pros and cons of registering the spouse-led PH operating entity as a DTI Sole Proprietorship versus an SEC One Person Corporation (OPC). It is designed to be a reference guide for the upcoming PH legal counsel consultation.

**Disclaimer:** This is research material, not legal or tax advice. The final vehicle choice must be vetted by counsel, especially considering the Anti-Dummy Law and the specific B2B vendor relationship with the US LLC.

---

## 1. DTI Sole Proprietorship

The simplest form of business structure in the Philippines. The spouse and the business are considered the same legal entity.

### Pros
- **Simplicity & Speed:** The fastest and easiest structure to register. Minimal initial requirements compared to the SEC.
- **Lower Costs:** Generally lower registration fees, LGUs fees, and fewer ongoing administrative/compliance requirements (no need to submit annual financial statements to SEC).
- **Taxation Options:** If non-VAT, eligible for the 8% flat income tax rate on gross sales/receipts (under TRAIN Law) in lieu of graduated income tax and percentage tax, making tax filing very simple for low-margin or service businesses.
- **Full Control:** The registered spouse has absolute authority without corporate protocols.

### Cons
- **Unlimited Liability (Critical Risk):** The business and the spouse are one. If the business is sued or accrues debt, the spouse's personal assets (and potentially conjugal properties) are fully exposed and at risk.
- **Succession:** The business ceases to exist upon the death or incapacitation of the registered owner. It cannot be easily transferred to another person without closing the DTI registration and starting over.
- **Credibility:** Sometimes viewed as less "professional" by enterprise clients or larger B2B vendors compared to a corporation, though less of an issue if acting primarily as a merchant of record for consumer SaaS.

---

## 2. One Person Corporation (OPC)

A corporation with a single stockholder (the spouse, who acts as the sole director and president). Registered with the SEC.

### Pros
- **Limited Liability (Major Benefit):** The OPC is a separate juridical entity. The spouse's personal assets are protected. Liability is limited to the assets/capital contributed to the OPC.
- **Corporate Perpetuity & Succession:** The OPC exists independently of the owner. Upon death or incapacity, a designated nominee steps in, allowing the business to continue seamlessly.
- **Professional Image:** Carries corporate credibility, which can be advantageous for banking, larger PSP limits, and B2B contracts.
- **Scalability:** Easier to convert to a regular corporation later if bringing in future partners or investors.

### Cons
- **Higher Compliance & Costs:** Must comply with strict SEC reporting requirements, including submitting audited annual financial statements and maintaining formal corporate records (minutes, books).
- **Corporate Taxation:** Subject to Regular Corporate Income Tax (RCIT) at 20% (if assets ≤ ₱100M and net income ≤ ₱5M) or 25%. Cannot avail of the 8% flat tax rate available to sole proprietors.
- **Setup Complexity:** Requires drafting Articles of Incorporation and appointing a nominee and alternate nominee.

## 3. Key Discussion Points for PH Counsel

When discussing these options with counsel, present the following specific scenarios:

1. **Anti-Dummy Law Risk Profile:** Does an OPC offer any stronger "veil" against Anti-Dummy scrutiny than a DTI, given that the OPC is still a 100% Filipino-owned entity but has corporate formalities?
2. **Intercompany Liability:** Since the US LLC will be contracting with the PH Entity, if the PH Entity breaches the contract or fails to pay the US LLC, does a DTI expose HitM's conjugal properties to their own US LLC's claims? Does an OPC perfectly ring-fence this?
3. **BMBE Exemption Interaction:** How do DTI vs. OPC structures interact practically with obtaining a BMBE Certificate of Authority? Is one favored by LGUs over the other?
4. **Compliance Burden vs. Revenue:** At what MRR does the cost of maintaining an OPC (audit fees, SEC reporting) become negligible compared to the liability protection it offers?
